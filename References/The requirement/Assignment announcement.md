## What Is the Baseline Model?

### Mission Brief: The Great Cat Census

Across campus, reports of mysterious cat sightings have been flooding in. Unfortunately, every eyewitness tells a different story: some claim they saw a Persian, others insist it was a Ragdoll, and nobody can agree on what was actually there.

As it turns out, eyewitness testimony is unreliable.

Your team has been tasked with deploying a remotely operated reconnaissance robot to conduct the Great Cat Census.

### Mission objectives

1. Navigate the survey area using remote control.
2. Search for every cat hidden around the environment.
3. Correctly identify each cat's breed using your deep learning model.
4. Report your findings to mission control (your laptop).
5. Return safely to the starting point before time runs out.

This may sound like a simple mission, but it represents something much bigger. Successfully completing the Great Cat Census demonstrates that you have integrated robotics, networking, computer vision, and deep learning into a single intelligent system. This is the first major milestone of the course. Once you have mastered this challenge, you will be ready to tackle your much more ambitious advanced project.

## Baseline Model Requirements

By Friday, your robot should be capable of:

- Driving reliably under remote control.
- Streaming live video from its onboard camera.
- Running your trained deep learning model on the live camera feed.
- Correctly identifying the breed of every cat it finds.
- Displaying the predicted breed on the console.
- Completing the course and returning to the starting position.

Starting tomorrow morning, mysterious cat sightings will begin appearing throughout ES1, ES2, and Makers@SoC. Use them to test your robot early and often.

## Team Priorities

- **Deep Learning team:** Focus on training and deploying a model that can accurately recognise the five cat species from the live camera feed. Complete the uploaded task for the next two days and fill out the required sheet.
- **Robotics team:** Ensure the robot can be driven reliably while streaming video.
- **All team members:** Start integrating the two systems as soon as possible. Integration is often the hardest part of any engineering project: software and hardware may already work individually, but getting everything to work together reliably and repeatedly takes time. Do not leave it until Thursday night.
